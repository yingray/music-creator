## Notes

Japanese post-rock with subtle math rock influence. The key aesthetic is restraint — atmospheric textures from post-rock, rhythmic precision from math rock, but neither element dominates. The user's original prompt was excellently written; preserved nearly verbatim with minimal v5 optimization.

### Key Design Decisions
- "instrumental Japanese post-rock with subtle math rock" — math rock is a modifier, not the lead genre
- "no drum showboating" — brilliant anti-pattern anchor from the original; critical for math rock where drums often dominate
- "restrained drums with ghost notes and rim clicks" — extremely specific drum direction that prevents the default rock drum pattern
- "atmospheric yet precise" — captures the core post-rock/math-rock tension
- BPM 120 — post-rock/math-rock mid-range; supports the "slow build" character with forward momentum

### Key Descriptors
- `clean bright guitar arpeggios and tapping`, `melodic bass lines`
- `ghost notes and rim clicks`, `gentle syncopation`, `slow build narrative arc`
- `no drum showboating`, `atmospheric yet precise`

### The Post-Rock / Math-Rock Balance
Post-rock contributes: atmosphere, spacious mix, slow build narrative, emotional depth
Math rock contributes: rhythmic precision, syncopation, tapping techniques, structural complexity
The prompt resolves this tension by leading with post-rock's atmosphere and using math rock as texture/technique.

### Custom Negative Prompt Strategy
More terms than typical non-classical (~23) because this niche genre needs protection from multiple drift directions:
- **Intensity drift:** heavy distortion, screaming, blast beats, double kick, metal, hardcore, aggressive
- **Genre confusion:** lo-fi, trap, hip-hop, EDM, classical, orchestral, acoustic folk, country, jazz fusion
- **Showmanship drift:** shred guitar, drum solo, chaotic, noisy
- **Vocal drift:** vocals, singing, voice (triple reinforcement for instrumental)

### v5 Notes
- v5 handles "ghost notes and rim clicks" as specific drum direction — produces more nuanced drum programming
- "slow build narrative arc" benefits from v5's 4-min pass length
- No metatags needed — the organic build is better served by pure descriptive prompting
