## v5 Fusion Pair Demo

Demonstrates v5's improved **fusion pair stability** — Gospel + Trap is a combination that v4 struggled with but v5 handles reliably.

### Why These Terms

- **140 BPM** — sits in the trap range (130–150, half-time feel); the gospel elements ride on top
- **"808 bass in key"** — specifying "in key" helps v5 keep the bass melodic and tuned
- **"gospel trap fusion"** — v5 processes fusion pairs as a single concept rather than competing genres
- **"powerful belted vocals with rap verses"** — describes the vocal arc: rap for verses, belted for chorus
- **"Hammond organ"** — specific instrument reference anchors the gospel side

### Structural Notes

- **`[Verse: Rap]` → `[Belted]` chorus** — the contrast between rap delivery and full-voice gospel chorus is the core of this fusion
- **`[Spoken Word]` bridge** — drops energy before the `[Drop]` for maximum impact
- **`[Drop]` + `[Choir: Gospel]` + `[Heavy Bass]`** — combining these creates the signature moment where trap production meets gospel power
- **`[Ad-lib: Yeah!]`** — gospel-style call-and-response element

### v5 Fusion Stability Notes

In v4, stacking Gospel + Trap often resulted in one genre dominating. v5 improvements:
- Fusion pairs are processed as a unified style concept
- Instrument tags manifest with clearer separation (808 stays crisp even with organ)
- Persona maintains consistent rap flow even when switching to belted chorus
