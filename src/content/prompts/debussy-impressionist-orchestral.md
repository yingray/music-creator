## Notes

Debussy-style Impressionist orchestral. The descriptive phrasing is kept more sentence-like than other classical prompts because Debussy's music IS about color and atmosphere — the evocative language ("harmonies blur and dissolve", "music feels suspended in time") actually works well as v5 prompt terms for this specific style.

Reference point: *Prelude to the Afternoon of a Faun* — iconic solo flute opening, gradually expanding orchestral colors, suspended harmonic world.

### Key Distinction from Ghibli Prompt
- Ghibli is "nature/spiritual" → this is "sensual/atmospheric"
- Ghibli builds to majestic climax → this stays in soft dynamics throughout
- Both share transparent orchestration, but Debussy's is more about color dissolving than layered building

### Key Descriptors
- `orchestral Impressionism`, `solo flute motif`, `harmonies blur and dissolve`
- `French fin-de-siecle`, `coloristic orchestration`, `shimmering harmonic washes`
- `music feels suspended in time`, `transparent layering`

### Effective Patterns
- "Debussy-inspired but fully original" — parallels "Rachmaninoff-inspired but fully original"
- "early 20th-century Impressionist idiom" — era anchor (specifically NOT "19th-century"; Debussy is early 20th)
- "strings enter gradually without sharp attacks" — technique-level instruction that shapes orchestral behavior
- "no percussion emphasis" — positive prompt exclusion reinforced by negative prompt
- "French fin-de-siecle aesthetic" — strong cultural anchor

### v5 Notes
- v5 handles the descriptive/evocative phrasing well — "harmonies blur and dissolve" produces genuinely Impressionist results
- "early 20th-century Impressionist idiom" prevents the late-Romantic drift that otherwise occurs with orchestral prompts
- v5's dynamic control respects "soft dynamics throughout" more reliably than v4
