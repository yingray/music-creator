## Notes

A progressive metal ballad in the spirit of Dream Theater — starts intimate with piano, builds through layered instrumentation to epic climax. The "70s prog rock influence" era anchor is more effective than naming specific bands.

### Key Design Decisions
- "symphonic progressive metal ballad" — genre leads, with "ballad" setting the emotional character
- "70s prog rock influence" — temporal reference > artist name (v5 best practice)
- "complex time signatures" — central to prog metal identity; preserved from original
- "80 BPM building to intensity" — starts as a ballad but allows acceleration
- "polished studio production" — prog metal requires clean, detailed production (not raw/lo-fi)
- "powerful emotional male vocal" — Dream Theater's ballads feature clean powerful vocals

### Key Descriptors
- `symphonic progressive metal`, `soaring`, `complex time signatures`, `virtuosic`
- `grand piano foundation`, `layered electric guitar`, `expressive violin`
- `70s prog rock influence`, `wide dynamic range`

### The Ballad-vs-Prog Tension
The prompt must balance "ballad" (slow, emotional, intimate) with "progressive metal" (complex, virtuosic, intense). The metatags manage this by controlling energy across sections — starting intimate and building to explosive.

### v5 Notes
- v5 handles the genre fusion (symphonic + progressive + metal + ballad) well when anchored with clear temporal reference
- "complex time signatures" produces more convincing odd-meter passages in v5
- Metatags are essential for this piece — without them, Suno may default to standard verse-chorus structure without the prog build
